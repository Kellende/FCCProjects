Ray Moore: a Man of Many Talents


Ray Moore was born in 1942 in Oklahoma and was, along with his mother, enrolled in the Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma. Mr. Moore long had an interest in wildlife and the workings of nature, and pursued higher education at Peninsula College in Port Angeles, Washington. Ray decided to study fisheries in particular, but eventually went on to work with the United States Forest Service for several years. Ray acquired a variety of professional accolades over the course of his three-decade career in this capacity, and wrote about issues in his field for several local news publications.


Ray spent much of his retirement being an active part of his community and working on several projects, particularly compiling and writing a history of his family and their Native American tradition and heritage. Mr. Moore is survived by his wife of 56 years, Nellie Moore, and several grandchildren, who remain pillars of their community and role models for those around them.
